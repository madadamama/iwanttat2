An interview with

How did you become interested in tattooing?

I’ve been an artist my whole life and I always knew I wanted a career in a creative field. I grew up painting and drawing and then studied graphic design in college. I was never that passionate about graphic design, but didn’t know what else to do. I started getting tattoos in college by a lot of self-taught artists. I thought it was the coolest thing and had the thought that if I really wanted to do it I probably could, but it seemed so far fetched at the time.

Fast forward a little, I graduated college and had my first graphic design job and I was miserable (surprise!). I couldn’t see myself having a full time office job for the rest of my life, and the idea of becoming a tattoo artist never left my mind. I thought why not, cause at this point I really had nothing to lose.

Coincidentally, I started when COVID hit. I tattooed inconsistently for about 2 years, just practicing on myself, friends, and family. It wasn’t until April of 2022 I decided to quit my design job and see if I could make it tattooing. I’m now a full time tattoo artist in a studio with some friends I have met along the way!

What advice would you give to someone who’s getting their first tattoo?

Researching artists is the most important part in my opinion. You’re trusting someone to put something permanent on your body! I always like when my clients tell me it’s their first tattoo so I can give them some more information about my booking and tattooing process. Eat a good meal and drinks lots of water beforehand. Don’t be afraid to ask questions and voice your concerns during your appointment. As I said, you’re putting so much trust into your artist!

Do you have a dream tattoo, if so, what does it look like?

My dream tattoo is a full body piece covering my backside from the artist Jess Chen (@__jesschen__). She does a lot of large scale floral pieces and it is my goal to be tattooed by her. I want it to start on the back of my legs and go all the way up to my back. Her use of both black and color are so insanely beautiful.
